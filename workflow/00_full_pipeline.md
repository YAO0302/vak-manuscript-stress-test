# Full Pipeline

This workflow describes the full manuscript preparation process.

## Stage 1. Target journal article upload

The user collects several articles from the target ВАК journal.

Recommended number: 3–10 articles.

The articles are used only for style analysis. They should not be copied, redistributed, or committed to this repository.

## Stage 2. Journal style profile extraction

The AI analyzes the selected articles and extracts:

* article structure;
* abstract pattern;
* introduction logic;
* methodology language;
* argumentation model;
* conclusion pattern;
* common academic phrases;
* citation behavior;
* sentence-level style;
* terminology density.

The output is a reusable journal style profile.

## Stage 3. Draft generation or revision

The user provides research topic, research question, object, subject, aim, tasks, methods, materials, and expected contribution.

The AI generates or revises the manuscript according to the journal style profile.

## Stage 4. Reference reality audit

The AI checks whether references appear real, internally consistent, and properly connected to in-text citations.

Every unverifiable source must be marked as requiring external verification.

The system must not invent missing sources.

## Stage 5. Citation-claim alignment check

The AI checks whether cited sources actually support the claims attached to them.

High-risk claims, unsupported generalizations, and citation misuse are flagged.

## Stage 6. Hostile peer review

The AI simulates strict journal reviewers and identifies:

* method-question mismatch;
* logical breaks;
* weak novelty;
* identification gaps;
* unsupported conclusions;
* possible rejection reasons.

## Stage 7. Structural reconstruction

The AI proposes a new structure for the manuscript.

The output includes:

* revised article logic;
* recommended section order;
* paragraph-level tasks;
* sections requiring rewriting;
* claims requiring evidence.

## Stage 8. Argument-forward rewriting

The manuscript is rewritten without excessive defensive formulas.

The text should directly develop the argument, avoid unnecessary disclaimers, and preserve clarity for first-time readers.

## Stage 9. Final submission check

The AI checks whether the manuscript is coherent, readable, structurally complete, and aligned with the target journal profile.
