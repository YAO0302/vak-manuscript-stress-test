# Logical Break Checklist

This checklist is used to detect logical breaks in Russian-language academic manuscripts prepared for ВАК journals and related scholarly publications.

A logical break occurs when the article moves from one idea to another without sufficient explanation, evidence, method, or transition.

## Core rule

A manuscript should not force the reader to guess the connection between claims.

Every major claim must be connected to:

* the research question;
* the method;
* the material;
* the evidence;
* the previous paragraph;
* the next analytical step;
* the conclusion.

If the connection is missing, the article has a logical break.

## 1. Main logic chain check

A strong manuscript should follow this chain:

```text id="f6y5sk"
Research problem
↓
Research question
↓
Object and subject
↓
Aim
↓
Tasks
↓
Material
↓
Method
↓
Analysis
↓
Findings
↓
Conclusion
```

Check:

* Does each element appear in the manuscript?
* Does each element follow from the previous one?
* Does the conclusion return to the research question?
* Does the article skip any necessary step?

Status:

```text id="dod94q"
[ ] No break
[ ] Minor break
[ ] Major break
[ ] Requires structural reconstruction
```

## 2. Relevance to research problem break

Problem:

The manuscript begins with a broad topic but does not identify a specific research problem.

Weak pattern:

```text id="noid7d"
Traditional culture is important. Architecture is important. Active longevity is important.
```

Logical break:

The reader does not know what exact problem the article solves.

Fix:

State the unresolved academic issue.

Stronger pattern:

```text id="95n1ja"
The problem lies in clarifying how traditional cultural elements change function when included in contemporary architectural environments associated with active longevity practices.
```

Check:

```text id="ig3zxp"
Does the introduction move from general relevance to a specific research problem?
```

## 3. Literature review to research gap break

Problem:

The manuscript lists sources but does not explain what remains unresolved.

Weak pattern:

```text id="hmgbk5"
Author A studied culture. Author B studied architecture. Author C studied active longevity.
```

Logical break:

The literature review does not produce the research gap.

Fix:

Group sources by approach and identify what they do not explain.

Stronger pattern:

```text id="xjs9ec"
Existing studies can be grouped into three directions. However, these approaches do not fully explain how traditional cultural elements are transformed into spatial components of contemporary architectural environments.
```

Check:

```text id="wy6dz9"
Does the literature review clearly lead to the research gap?
```

## 4. Research gap to aim break

Problem:

The manuscript states a gap but the aim does not address it.

Example:

Research gap:

```text id="zhgq7s"
The transformation of traditional cultural elements in contemporary architecture remains insufficiently studied.
```

Weak aim:

```text id="krwyuy"
The aim is to study active longevity.
```

Logical break:

The aim does not follow from the gap.

Fix:

Make the aim solve the gap.

Stronger aim:

```text id="4yrm27"
The aim is to identify the ways in which traditional cultural elements are transformed in contemporary architectural environments and to determine how these elements participate in shaping spatial conditions associated with active longevity practices.
```

Check:

```text id="t7qal3"
Does the aim directly respond to the stated research gap?
```

## 5. Aim to tasks break

Problem:

The article states a broad aim, but the tasks do not lead to it.

Weak tasks:

```text id="bcpzif"
1. Study the topic.
2. Consider the problem.
3. Draw conclusions.
```

Logical break:

The tasks are decorative and do not organize the article.

Fix:

Make each task correspond to a real section or analytical step.

Stronger tasks:

```text id="dc8c8z"
1. Define traditional cultural elements in relation to architectural space.
2. Explain the method used to analyze their transformation.
3. Identify the selected material and selection criteria.
4. Analyze the transformation of these elements in contemporary architecture.
5. Interpret their relation to spatial conditions associated with active longevity practices.
```

Check:

```text id="2mnxfh"
Does each task produce a visible part of the manuscript?
```

## 6. Method to research question break

Problem:

The method is listed but not connected to the question.

Weak pattern:

```text id="u5hf0z"
The article uses comparative, semiotic, and historical-cultural methods.
```

Logical break:

The reader does not know why these methods are suitable.

Fix:

Explain what each method does.

Stronger pattern:

```text id="hdzlsy"
Comparative analysis is used to identify differences between traditional and contemporary architectural forms; semiotic analysis makes it possible to interpret cultural elements as signs; historical-cultural analysis connects these signs to cultural memory and continuity.
```

Check:

```text id="qbmgrn"
Can the selected method answer the research question?
```

## 7. Material to method break

Problem:

The article names methods but does not define the material to which they are applied.

Logical break:

A method cannot operate without material.

Check:

* What is being analyzed?
* Are the cases, texts, images, documents, or spaces named?
* Are selection criteria explained?
* Can the method actually analyze this material?

If material is missing, mark:

```text id="rxafgg"
[AUTHOR MUST PROVIDE MATERIAL]
```

Fix:

Add a material section.

Template:

```text id="a3g0ha"
Материалом исследования выступают [material], выбранные с учетом [selection criteria].
```

## 8. Description to analysis break

Problem:

The manuscript describes examples but does not explain what they show.

Weak pattern:

```text id="m9ibmj"
The building contains traditional elements. The project uses Chinese motifs. The space is visually attractive.
```

Logical break:

Description does not become analysis.

Fix:

After presenting material, explain its analytical meaning.

Stronger pattern:

```text id="8m82r4"
The traditional element functions not only as a visual reference, but also as a spatial marker that organizes movement, orientation, or zones of social interaction.
```

Check:

```text id="l02rh5"
Does each example support a specific analytical claim?
```

## 9. Analysis to interpretation break

Problem:

The manuscript analyzes material but does not explain why the result matters.

Logical break:

The reader sees local observations but not their significance.

Fix:

Connect each analytical result to the research question.

Template:

```text id="0bwd5a"
This finding is significant because it shows [connection to research question].
```

Russian formula:

```text id="c2yxkr"
Данный результат позволяет уточнить [connection to research question].
```

Check:

```text id="g2if1l"
Does the analysis explain the significance of the finding?
```

## 10. Cultural element to active longevity break

Problem:

The manuscript moves directly from traditional cultural elements to active longevity.

Weak pattern:

```text id="ex4gzn"
Traditional Chinese elements are used in architecture. Therefore, they support active longevity.
```

Logical break:

The mechanism is missing.

Fix:

Introduce an intermediate chain.

Recommended chain:

```text id="hinoro"
traditional cultural element
↓
architectural form
↓
spatial organization
↓
social interaction / mobility / orientation
↓
everyday practices
↓
active longevity context
```

Stronger pattern:

```text id="i72tcy"
Traditional cultural elements can be analyzed as part of architectural environments that support social interaction, mobility, orientation, and everyday practices associated with active longevity.
```

Check:

```text id="si4qdu"
Does the manuscript explain the mechanism connecting architecture to active longevity?
```

## 11. Evidence to claim break

Problem:

The manuscript makes a claim that the evidence does not support.

Example:

Evidence:

```text id="0m0fpm"
One building includes traditional decorative motifs.
```

Unsupported claim:

```text id="8p31m9"
Traditional Chinese architecture improves active longevity.
```

Logical break:

The evidence is too narrow and does not support the claim.

Fix:

Narrow the claim or add appropriate evidence.

Stronger claim:

```text id="j1w68a"
In the analyzed case, traditional decorative motifs participate in the visual and symbolic organization of the architectural environment.
```

Check:

```text id="h85bg9"
Does the evidence support the exact claim being made?
```

## 12. Citation to claim break

Problem:

A source is cited, but it does not support the attached claim.

Logical break:

The citation creates a false appearance of support.

Check:

* Does the source actually say what the manuscript claims?
* Is the source from the correct field?
* Does the citation support the exact sentence?
* Is the citation decorative?
* Is the reference real and verified?

If uncertain, mark:

```text id="o4wq6k"
[SOURCE REQUIRED]
[VERIFY REFERENCE]
[VERIFY DOI]
```

Fix:

* narrow the claim;
* replace with a verified relevant source;
* remove the citation;
* remove the unsupported claim.

## 13. Method to conclusion break

Problem:

The method cannot support the conclusion.

Example:

Method:

```text id="unbppg"
Semiotic analysis of architectural symbols.
```

Unsupported conclusion:

```text id="m5e4e1"
Architecture directly improves the health of older adults.
```

Logical break:

Semiotic analysis cannot prove direct health outcomes.

Fix:

Narrow the conclusion.

Stronger conclusion:

```text id="1gd2ck"
The analysis shows that architectural symbols can participate in the formation of spatial environments associated with social interaction, orientation, and everyday practices.
```

Check:

```text id="vfr3xc"
Does the conclusion stay within the limits of the method?
```

## 14. Section order break

Problem:

The manuscript introduces conclusions before evidence, methods after analysis, or theory after it is used.

Check:

* Does the introduction establish the problem before technical detail?
* Does the method appear before or at the start of analysis?
* Are key concepts defined before use?
* Does evidence appear before conclusion?
* Does the conclusion avoid new concepts?

Fix:

Reorder sections.

Recommended sequence:

```text id="pu35sr"
Introduction
↓
Literature review / theoretical framework
↓
Methodology and material
↓
Main analysis
↓
Discussion or interpretation
↓
Conclusion
```

## 15. Paragraph transition break

Problem:

Paragraphs are individually understandable but do not connect to each other.

Check:

* Does the first sentence connect to the previous paragraph?
* Does the last sentence prepare the next paragraph?
* Is there a clear transition between literature, method, and analysis?
* Does the paragraph sequence follow the argument?

Useful Russian transition formulas:

* Это позволяет перейти к...
* В связи с этим необходимо рассмотреть...
* Следовательно, дальнейший анализ должен учитывать...
* Данный вывод подготавливает анализ...
* На этом основании можно перейти к...

## 16. Concept consistency break

Problem:

The manuscript uses several related terms as if they were identical.

High-risk terms:

* traditional culture;
* cultural heritage;
* cultural memory;
* cultural code;
* national tradition;
* architectural environment;
* public space;
* active longevity;
* active aging;
* healthy aging.

Logical break:

The argument becomes unstable because key concepts shift.

Fix:

Define the main terms and keep them consistent.

Check:

```text id="nxhm2s"
Are key terms used consistently throughout the manuscript?
```

## 17. Scale break

Problem:

The article moves from a small case to a broad conclusion.

Example:

Material:

```text id="8qjltb"
One architectural object.
```

Conclusion:

```text id="4kr5ly"
All contemporary Chinese architecture transforms traditional culture in this way.
```

Logical break:

The scale of evidence does not match the scale of conclusion.

Fix:

Narrow the conclusion.

Stronger:

```text id="20hd1d"
The analyzed case demonstrates one possible way in which traditional cultural elements can be transformed in contemporary architectural space.
```

Check:

```text id="g39tbg"
Does the conclusion match the scale of the material?
```

## 18. Causal leap break

Problem:

The article moves from association to causation.

High-risk words:

* causes;
* ensures;
* proves;
* directly influences;
* determines;
* improves;
* leads to;
* обеспечивает;
* доказывает;
* определяет;
* напрямую влияет;
* приводит к.

Fix:

Use causal language only when evidence supports it.

If evidence is not causal, use:

* is associated with;
* can be considered through;
* forms conditions for;
* participates in;
* may support;
* can be interpreted as.

Example:

Weak:

```text id="qh4c2t"
Архитектура обеспечивает активное долголетие.
```

Stronger:

```text id="56agh1"
Архитектурная среда формирует условия, в которых могут поддерживаться социальная активность, мобильность и устойчивые повседневные практики.
```

## 20. Repair actions

Use one or more repair actions:

* add a missing transition;
* define a key concept;
* narrow a claim;
* add evidence;
* remove unsupported claim;
* move methodology earlier;
* clarify material;
* explain selection criteria;
* rewrite conclusion;
* rebuild section order;
* add field-appropriate sources;
* remove decorative citations;
* replace defensive wording with positive analytical framing.

## 21. Final decision

Choose one decision.

```text id="9hbo4f"
No major logical breaks detected
```

Use only if the manuscript chain is stable.

```text id="sd5fje"
Minor logical repair required
```

Use if only transitions or wording need correction.

```text id="2unl57"
Major logical reconstruction required
```

Use if method, material, analysis, or conclusion do not connect.

```text id="1jbjko"
Do not submit
```

Use if the manuscript contains unsupported causal claims, unverifiable evidence, or conclusions that exceed the method.

## Final checklist

Before submission, confirm:

* the research problem is specific;
* the literature review produces the gap;
* the aim follows from the gap;
* tasks lead to the aim;
* method fits the question;
* material fits the method;
* analysis goes beyond description;
* interpretation connects to the question;
* claims match evidence;
* citations support claims;
* active longevity claims have a clear mechanism;
* conclusion matches the method and material;
* no major causal leap remains;
* no broad conclusion is based on narrow material.

## Final rule

A logical break is not fixed by better wording.

It is fixed by restoring the missing connection between problem, method, evidence, and conclusion.
