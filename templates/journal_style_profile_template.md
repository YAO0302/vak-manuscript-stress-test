Recommended number of articles: 3–10.

The profile must describe high-level academic writing patterns only. It must not copy, reproduce, or imitate individual authors.

## 3. Title pattern

Analyze the titles of the target journal articles.

Describe:

* average length;
* whether titles are descriptive or conceptual;
* whether titles use a colon;
* whether titles mention method, material, or object;
* whether titles are narrow or broad.

Recommended title formula:

[Concept / process] in [object/material/context]: [analytical focus]

Possible title patterns:

* [Concept] as a factor of [process]
* Transformation of [phenomenon] in [context]
* [Object] in the context of [theoretical frame]
* [Phenomenon]: [historical-cultural / architectural / philosophical interpretation]

## 4. Abstract pattern

Describe the typical abstract structure.

Check whether the abstract contains:

* актуальность;
* problem statement;
* aim;
* material;
* method;
* result;
* novelty;
* significance.

Recommended abstract structure:

Sentence 1: актуальность.

Sentence 2: research problem or gap.

Sentence 3: aim of the article.

Sentence 4: material and methods.

Sentence 5: main result.

Sentence 6: scientific significance or contribution.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* Актуальность исследования обусловлена...
* В статье рассматривается...
* Цель статьи состоит в...
* Материалом исследования послужили...
* Методологическую основу исследования составляют...
* В результате исследования выявлено...
* Научная значимость статьи заключается в...

## 6. Introduction pattern

Describe how introductions are usually written.

Check whether the introduction includes:

* broad disciplinary context;
* актуальность;
* research gap;
* literature positioning;
* object and subject;
* aim and tasks;
* methodology;
* article structure.

Recommended introduction sequence:

1. Introduce the broader academic problem.
2. Explain why the problem is relevant now.
3. Identify the research gap.
4. Position the manuscript in relation to existing literature.
5. Define the object and subject of research.
6. State the aim.
7. List the tasks.
8. Explain the methods and material.
9. Transition to the analysis.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* В современной гуманитарной науке особое значение приобретает...
* Актуальность обращения к данной проблеме определяется...
* Несмотря на наличие исследований, посвященных..., недостаточно изученным остается...
* Объектом исследования является...
* Предметом исследования выступает...
* Цель статьи состоит в...
* Для достижения поставленной цели решаются следующие задачи...
* Методологическую основу исследования составляют...

## 7. Methodology pattern

Describe how methodology is usually presented.

Check:

* whether methods are explicitly named;
* whether the method is explained or only listed;
* whether methods are linked to research material;
* whether the article uses humanities methods, empirical methods, comparative methods, semiotic methods, historical-cultural analysis, discourse analysis, or other approaches.

Recommended rule:

The method must naturally follow from the research question.

Avoid abrupt transitions from broad theoretical discussion to method announcement.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* Поскольку исследование направлено на..., целесообразным является использование...
* Выбор метода обусловлен необходимостью...
* Сравнительный анализ позволяет выявить...
* Семиотический анализ дает возможность рассмотреть...
* Историко-культурный подход позволяет проследить...
* Междисциплинарный характер исследования требует сочетания...

## 8. Theoretical framework pattern

Describe how the target journal uses theory.

Check:

* whether theory is central or auxiliary;
* whether concepts are defined explicitly;
* whether theoretical sources are discussed or only cited;
* how theory is connected to the material.

Recommended structure:

1. Define the key concept.
2. Identify the theoretical tradition.
3. Explain why this theory is relevant.
4. Apply the theory to the material.
5. Avoid decorative theory.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* Теоретической основой исследования является...
* В рамках настоящей статьи понятие ... рассматривается как...
* Данный подход позволяет интерпретировать...
* Концепт ... используется для анализа...

## 9. Argumentation model

Describe how the journal articles usually build arguments.

Possible models:

* historical-cultural model;
* comparative model;
* typological model;
* semiotic model;
* hermeneutic model;
* architectural-spatial model;
* interdisciplinary model;
* case-based model.

Recommended argument sequence:

1. Define the problem.
2. Introduce the analytical category.
3. Present the material.
4. Analyze the material through the selected method.
5. Interpret the results.
6. Return to the research question.
7. Formulate contribution.

## 10. Citation behavior

Describe the journal's citation habits.

Check:

* density of citations;
* Russian vs international sources;
* recent vs classical sources;
* use of direct quotations;
* use of paraphrase;
* placement of citations;
* citation style.

Recommended citation rules:

* Do not use references as decoration.
* Do not cite a source unless it supports the claim.
* Do not allow bibliography entries that are not cited in the text.
* Do not cite unverifiable sources.
* Do not invent references or DOI numbers.
* Mark every uncertain reference for manual verification.

## 11. Sentence-level style

Describe the preferred sentence style.

Check:

* average sentence length;
* use of nominalizations;
* use of passive constructions;
* use of impersonal constructions;
* use of transition formulas;
* degree of caution;
* degree of directness.

Recommended style:

* formal;
* precise;
* analytical;
* non-colloquial;
* direct;
* not overloaded with defensive explanations.

Useful Russian formulas:

* Это позволяет рассматривать...
* Данный подход выявляет...
* Анализ показывает...
* Особое значение приобретает...
* В результате можно выделить...
* Следовательно, ...
* Таким образом, ...

## 12. Paragraph structure

Each paragraph should perform one clear task.

Recommended paragraph functions:

* introduce a problem;
* define a concept;
* summarize literature;
* formulate a gap;
* present method;
* analyze material;
* interpret result;
* connect evidence to claim;
* conclude a section.

Avoid paragraphs that:

* repeat the same claim;
* combine too many tasks;
* introduce methods too early;
* answer objections before developing the argument;
* contain technical detail without context.

## 13. Conclusion pattern

Describe how conclusions are usually written.

Check whether conclusions:

* return to the aim;
* answer the research question;
* summarize findings;
* indicate novelty;
* indicate theoretical or practical significance;
* avoid unsupported generalization;
* avoid merely repeating the abstract.

Recommended conclusion sequence:

1. Return to the research aim.
2. State the main result.
3. Explain how the result was obtained.
4. Indicate scholarly contribution.
5. Define the limits of the conclusion only if necessary.
6. Suggest future research only if appropriate.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* Проведенный анализ позволяет заключить...
* В результате исследования установлено...
* Таким образом, ...
* Полученные результаты показывают...
* Научная значимость исследования состоит в...
* Дальнейшее изучение данной проблемы может быть связано с...

## 14. What to avoid

List features that would make a manuscript weak for this journal.

Possible risks:

* unclear research question;
* weak connection between method and problem;
* decorative theory;
* descriptive writing without analysis;
* unsupported generalizations;
* excessive defensive formulas;
* abrupt transitions;
* fabricated or unverifiable references;
* overuse of technical detail in the introduction;
* conclusion that exceeds the evidence;
* missing research gap;
* weak novelty.

## 15. Writing instructions for future manuscripts

Use this section as the reusable instruction block for generating or revising future manuscripts.

Template:

When writing or revising a manuscript for this journal:

1. Use formal Russian academic style.
2. Begin from the research problem, not from broad background alone.
3. State актуальность clearly.
4. Formulate the research question, aim, object, subject, and tasks consistently.
5. Link the method naturally to the research problem and material.
6. Use theory as an analytical tool, not as decoration.
7. Move from description to interpretation.
8. Support every major claim with a real and relevant source.
9. Avoid fabricated or unverifiable references.
10. Avoid defensive over-explanation.
11. Make each paragraph perform one clear task.
12. Keep the conclusion aligned with the aim and evidence.
