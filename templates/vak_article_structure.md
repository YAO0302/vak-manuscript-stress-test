# VAK Article Structure Template

This template provides a standard structure for a Russian-language academic article prepared for a ВАК journal or related scholarly publication.

It should be adapted to the target journal's author guidelines and the Journal Style Profile.

## Purpose

The purpose of this template is to help authors build a manuscript with clear Russian academic logic:

Research problem → актуальность → research gap → object → subject → aim → tasks → method → material → analysis → conclusion.

The template is especially suitable for humanities, cultural studies, philosophy, art history, architecture theory, and social sciences.

## Important rule

This template is not a substitute for the target journal's official author guidelines.

Before submission, always check:

* article length;
* abstract length;
* keyword requirements;
* citation style;
* bibliography style;
* section heading rules;
* originality requirements;
* formatting rules;
* submission rules.

## 1. Title

The title should identify the research object and analytical focus.

A strong title is:

* precise;
* not too broad;
* not journalistic;
* connected to the research problem;
* aligned with the target journal field.

Possible formulas:

* [Phenomenon] in the context of [theoretical or cultural frame]
* Transformation of [phenomenon] in [field / period / space]
* [Object] as a factor of [process]
* [Concept] in contemporary [disciplinary field]
* [Cultural element] in [architectural / social / historical context]: [analytical focus]

Russian formulas:

* Трансформация [явление] в [контекст]
* [Объект] в контексте [теоретическая рамка]
* [Понятие] как фактор [процесс]
* [Явление] в современной [область]: [аналитический фокус]
* [Культурный элемент] в [архитектурный / социальный / исторический контекст]

## 2. Author information

Follow the target journal requirements.

Common elements:

* full name;
* affiliation;
* city and country;
* email;
* ORCID, if required;
* academic status;
* funding information, if required.

Do not invent institutional information.

## 3. Abstract

The abstract should summarize the article's academic logic.

Recommended structure:

Sentence 1: актуальность.

Sentence 2: research problem or gap.

Sentence 3: aim of the article.

Sentence 4: material and methods.

Sentence 5: main result.

Sentence 6: scientific significance or contribution.

Russian formulas:

* Актуальность исследования обусловлена...
* В статье рассматривается...
* Цель статьи состоит в...
* Материалом исследования выступают...
* Методологическую основу исследования составляют...
* В результате исследования выявлено...
* Научная значимость статьи заключается в...

If results are not yet available, mark:

[RESULT REQUIRES EVIDENCE]

## 4. Keywords

Recommended number:

5–8 keywords, unless the journal requires otherwise.

Keyword types:

* core concept;
* research object;
* method;
* theoretical frame;
* cultural or geographical context;
* discipline-specific term.

Example structure:

```text
Ключевые слова: [понятие 1]; [понятие 2]; [объект]; [метод]; [контекст]; [дисциплинарный термин].
```

## 5. Introduction

The introduction should prepare the reader for the research problem.

Recommended functions:

1. Introduce the broader academic context.
2. Justify актуальность.
3. Identify the research gap.
4. Position the article in relation to existing literature.
5. Define object and subject, if required.
6. State aim and tasks.
7. Explain material and methods.
8. Transition to the main analysis.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* В современной гуманитарной науке особое значение приобретает...
* Актуальность обращения к данной проблеме определяется...
* Несмотря на наличие исследований, посвященных..., недостаточно изученным остается...
* Объектом исследования является...
* Предметом исследования выступает...
* Цель статьи состоит в...
* Для достижения поставленной цели решаются следующие задачи...
* Методологическую основу исследования составляют...

Avoid:

* overly broad historical background;
* decorative relevance;
* unclear research gap;
* method list without explanation;
* technical detail before the reader understands the problem;
* defensive formulas at the beginning.

## 6. Literature review

The literature review may be a separate section or part of the introduction, depending on the journal style.

Its purpose is not to list sources.

Its purpose is to show:

* what has already been studied;
* what approaches dominate the field;
* what remains unresolved;
* how the current article is positioned;
* why the current research is necessary.

Recommended structure:

1. Group sources by topic or approach.
2. Identify what each group contributes.
3. Identify the limitation or gap.
4. Connect the gap to the research question.

Avoid:

* listing authors without synthesis;
* citing sources that are not used;
* outdated literature without justification;
* decorative citation clusters;
* unsupported claim that the topic is insufficiently studied.

## 7. Research problem, object, subject, aim, and tasks

Some ВАК journals expect these elements explicitly.

Use them when appropriate.

### Research problem

The research problem should name the unresolved academic issue.

Formula:

```text
Исследовательская проблема заключается в...
```

### Object of research

The object is the broader field or phenomenon being studied.

Formula:

```text
Объектом исследования является...
```

### Subject of research

The subject is the specific aspect of the object analyzed in the article.

Formula:

```text
Предметом исследования выступает...
```

### Aim

The aim should follow from the research problem.

Formula:

```text
Цель статьи состоит в...
```

### Tasks

Tasks should lead to the aim and correspond to article sections.

Formula:

```text
Для достижения поставленной цели решаются следующие задачи:
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
```

Check consistency:

* object must be broader than subject;
* subject must be precise;
* aim must be answerable within one article;
* tasks must be concrete;
* tasks must not be decorative;
* conclusion must return to the aim.

## 8. Methodology and material

The methodology section should explain how the research is conducted.

It should not merely list methods.

Include:

* methods used;
* why these methods fit the research question;
* what material is analyzed;
* why this material was selected;
* how analysis is performed.

Possible methods for humanities and architecture-related research:

* сравнительный анализ;
* историко-культурный анализ;
* семиотический анализ;
* герменевтический анализ;
* типологический анализ;
* дискурс-анализ;
* case study;
* visual analysis;
* spatial analysis;
* interdisciplinary analysis.

Strong method connection formula:

```text
Поскольку исследование направлено на [research aim], метод [method] позволяет [analytical function].
```

Example:

```text
Поскольку исследование направлено на выявление способов трансформации традиционных культурных элементов в современной архитектурной форме, семиотический анализ позволяет рассмотреть эти элементы как систему знаков, связанных с исторической памятью, пространственной организацией и визуальной репрезентацией.
```

Avoid:

* naming methods that are not used;
* using method terminology as decoration;
* choosing methods that do not answer the research question;
* abrupt transitions from broad theory to method;
* claiming results the method cannot support.

## 9. Theoretical framework

Include a theoretical framework only if it helps the analysis.

The theoretical framework should:

* define key concepts;
* identify the analytical lens;
* explain why the lens fits the material;
* support the interpretation;
* connect to the main analysis.

Avoid:

* name-dropping;
* decorative theory;
* concepts that disappear after the introduction;
* theories that do not affect the analysis;
* mixing concepts from different fields without explanation.

Recommended formulas:

* Теоретической основой исследования является...
* В рамках настоящей статьи понятие ... рассматривается как...
* Данный подход позволяет интерпретировать...
* Концепт ... используется для анализа...

## 10. Main analysis

The main analysis is the core of the article.

Each analytical section should:

1. State a clear claim.
2. Present material or evidence.
3. Explain how the material is analyzed.
4. Interpret the result.
5. Connect the interpretation to the research question.

Recommended structure:

### Section 1: Conceptual or theoretical clarification

Function:

* define the key analytical category;
* explain how it will be used.

### Section 2: Material analysis

Function:

* analyze the selected material;
* apply the method;
* show evidence.

### Section 3: Interpretation

Function:

* explain what the analysis shows;
* connect findings to broader academic problem.

Adapt section titles to the target journal style.

Avoid:

* describing material without analysis;
* making broad cultural claims without evidence;
* claiming influence without showing mechanism;
* moving too quickly from architecture to health or social effects;
* repeating the same relevance claim;
* adding citations that do not support the claim.

## 11. Discussion

Some ВАК journals do not require a separate discussion section, especially in humanities.

Use this section only if it fits the target journal profile.

The discussion may:

* compare findings with previous research;
* explain theoretical significance;
* discuss implications;
* clarify contribution;
* identify limitations if necessary.

Avoid turning the discussion into defensive explanation.

## 12. Conclusion

The conclusion should complete the article's logic.

It should:

1. Return to the aim.
2. Answer the research question.
3. Summarize the main findings.
4. Explain the contribution.
5. Avoid claims not supported by the analysis.
6. Avoid introducing new sources or new concepts.

Recommended Russian formulas:

* Проведенный анализ позволяет заключить...
* В результате исследования установлено...
* Таким образом, ...
* Полученные результаты показывают...
* Научная значимость исследования состоит в...
* Дальнейшее изучение данной проблемы может быть связано с...

Avoid:

* simply repeating the abstract;
* introducing new evidence;
* broad conclusions unsupported by material;
* exaggerated novelty;
* defensive formulas.

## 13. References

Follow the target journal requirements.

Before submission, check:

* every source is real;
* DOI and bibliographic data are correct;
* every in-text citation has a bibliography entry;
* every bibliography entry is cited;
* every citation supports the claim attached to it;
* no fake or unverifiable references remain.

Never invent references.

Never invent DOI numbers.

Mark uncertain sources as:

[VERIFY REFERENCE]

## 14. Final pre-submission structure check

Use this checklist:

* Title is precise.
* Abstract contains relevance, aim, method, result, and contribution.
* Keywords reflect the article's field.
* Introduction states relevance and research gap.
* Object and subject are consistent.
* Aim and tasks are clear.
* Methods fit the research question.
* Material is defined.
* Literature review creates a gap.
* Main body analyzes, not only describes.
* Conclusion returns to the aim.
* References are verified.
* Citations support claims.
* Text avoids excessive defensive formulas.
* Each paragraph has a clear function.

## 15. Minimal article skeleton

```text
Title

Author information

Abstract

Keywords

Introduction

Literature review / State of research

Research problem, object, subject, aim, and tasks

Methodology and material

Theoretical framework

Main analysis

Discussion / Interpretation

Conclusion

References
```

Adapt this skeleton to the target journal profile.
